Natural Language Processing and Large Language Model Research

Experiences as a Undergraduate Researcher in AI/ML & NLP
More Info on DRAGN Labs

Experiences

  • I have worked in the Deep Representations and Architectures for Generative systems and Natural language understanding (DRAGN) Lab for the last 18 months, researching attention-based language model architectures.
  • I played a critical role in developing a model pipeline to pretrain one - three billion parameter models in parallel across multiple GPUs. This pipeline is a major advancement for the language modeling labs at BYU, enabling my team and other labs to study new LLM research areas beyond simply fine-tuning and prompt engineering effects.
  • The model training is currently complete, and this work is scheduled for submission as a full-length research paper at ACL 2025.
  • In addition, I created a comprehensive benchmarking suite for comparing the performance and capabilities of three different attention mechanisms.
  • The goal for this research is to distinguish the properties endowed by each attention mechanism and establish a methodology allowing for future practitioners to choose the most suitable architecture for their individual use case.
